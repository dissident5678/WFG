# Wright Foster Group LLC Automation Blockers

Purpose: checklist of access, credentials, authority, and systems needed before Hermes can run the SAM.gov opportunity-to-bid workflow with minimal blocking.

## 1. Company identity and registrations
- Legal entity: Wright Foster Group LLC.
- Formation state: Maryland.
- Domain: wrightfostergroup.com.
- LLC registration in Maryland completed or in progress with owner confirmation.
- Domain purchase completed or in progress with owner confirmation.
- Ownership/control documented: female veteran partner 51%; Nick 49%.
- EIN obtained.
- Business address and phone number selected.
- Business bank account opened.
- SAM.gov entity registration completed.
- UEI/CAGE issued.
- NAICS/PSC list selected.
- Core capability statement prepared.
- WOSB/EDWOSB and veteran-owned eligibility/certification path verified before any claims are made.

## 2. Email and domain
- Domain selected: wrightfostergroup.com.
- Business email created, ideally bids@wrightfostergroup.com or contracts@wrightfostergroup.com.
- Hermes email access configured for sending and reading outreach replies.
- Sent mail, inbox, and labels/folders working.
- Standing rules defined for what Hermes may send automatically.

## 3. Portal/API access
- SAM.gov login available to the authorized owner/operator.
- SAM.gov API key if using automated opportunity discovery.
- Login/password storage strategy selected for procurement portals.
- 2FA/CAPTCHA handling process defined, because some portals will still require human action.

## 4. Authority and approval rules
- Define whether Hermes can contact subcontractors without per-email approval.
- Define whether Hermes can submit clarification questions to contracting officers.
- Define final bid submission rule. Recommended: require explicit authorized-owner approval before any binding bid/proposal submission.
- Define max bid amount and max markup/discount authority.
- Define no-go rules for set-asides, licensing, bonding, insurance, and unclear scope.

## 5. Subcontractor sourcing and outreach
- Subcontractor CRM/database format selected.
- Outreach templates approved.
- Quote request form/template created.
- Follow-up cadence approved.
- Phone/SMS system optional but useful for faster quote collection.

## 6. Documents, signatures, and storage
- Standard company documents folder created.
- W-9, capability statement, insurance certificates, licenses, bonding docs, and certification documents stored when available.
- PDF editing/signing workflow selected.
- Proposal archive structure finalized.
- Optional Google Drive/Workspace integration configured if cloud docs are desired.

## 7. Pricing and risk controls
- Default overhead, profit, and contingency rules set.
- Minimum acceptable gross margin set.
- Bond/insurance/admin cost assumptions set.
- Quote comparison sheet/template prepared.
- Red-team checklist required before final submission.

## 8. Compliance guardrails
- Never claim certifications, set-aside eligibility, insurance, bonding, licenses, past performance, veteran-owned status, women-owned status, or subcontractor commitments unless verified.
- Never submit a bid until the compliance matrix and red-team review are complete.
- Flag legal, tax, bonding, insurance, or procurement interpretation questions for qualified professionals.
